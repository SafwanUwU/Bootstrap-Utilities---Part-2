The CPU, also known as the processor, is the brain of the computer. It carries out instructions from software by performing calculations and logic operations at lightning speed. CPUs have multiple cores that allow them to handle multiple tasks simultaneously, making modern computers faster and more efficient. The clock speed (GHz) determines how quickly a CPU processes data, and higher speeds mean better performance.

The motherboard is the central hub that connects all computer parts. It houses the CPU, RAM, GPU, and storage devices, allowing them to communicate efficiently. It also has ports & expansion slots for USB devices, WiFi adapters, and other upgrades. A high-quality motherboard ensures a stable and long-lasting system.

‚ö° RAM (Random Access Memory)

RAM is the short-term memory of a computer. It temporarily stores data that the CPU needs to access quickly. The more RAM a computer has, the more applications it can run smoothly at once. Unlike storage drives (HDD/SSD), RAM resets when the computer is turned off. More RAM = Faster multitasking & better performance!

Storage (SSD & HDD)

  • HDD (Hard Disk Drive): Uses spinning disks to read/write data, making it slower but cheaper with larger capacities.
  • SSD (Solid State Drive): Uses flash memory, making it much faster, more reliable, and energy-efficient. Upgrading to an SSD can significantly boost a computer‚Äôs speed!

The PSU converts AC power from the wall into DC power for the computer‚Äôs components. A high-quality PSU ensures stable voltage and protects hardware from power surges. Choosing a higher wattage PSU is important if you have a powerful GPU or multiple hard drives.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cooling System (Fans & Liquid Cooling)

Computers generate heat, especially when running heavy applications like gaming or video editing. Cooling systems include:

  • Air Cooling (Fans & Heatsinks): Most PCs use fans to pull in cool air and push out hot air.
  • Liquid Cooling: High-performance PCs use liquid cooling systems to keep the CPU & GPU cool.

Overheating can slow down performance or even damage components!

üñß Network Card (Ethernet & WiFi Adapter)

A network card connects a computer to the internet or local networks.

  • Ethernet Card: Provides a wired internet connection, which is faster & more stable.
  • WiFi Adapter: Allows wireless connectivity using WiFi signals. Some motherboards have built-in WiFi, while others need an external WiFi card or USB adapter.

üîä Sound Card

The sound card processes audio signals and improves sound quality. Most modern computers have integrated sound cards, but dedicated sound cards are used in music production and gaming for high-quality audio.

Optical drives are used to read/write CDs, DVDs, and Blu-ray discs. However, most modern computers no longer include them, as USB drives and cloud storage are more common.

BIOS/UEFI (Basic Input/Output System)

The BIOS (or UEFI in modern systems) is firmware stored on the motherboard. It controls hardware initialization when the computer starts up. The BIOS also allows users to modify settings such as boot order and system voltage.
